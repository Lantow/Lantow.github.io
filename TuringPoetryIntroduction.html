MILTON OR MASHINE?

EXPERIMENT INSTRUCTIONS:

First of all: THANK YOU FOR THE HELP AND PARTICIPATION!

In this experiment you are asked to assess both generated and real poetry.
The real poetry is random excerpts from John Milton's 1667 "Paradise Lost"
and the generated poetry is from a Generative Adversarial Neural Network
trained on the same text with different preprocessing methods.

You will not be told if the author of the verse is machine or human, as this is part of your task to asses and guess.

Besides guessing if the poetry is from a machine or from Milton,
you are asked to judge the poetry on two parameters: Grammaticality, and Poeticness.
The parameters are assessed on a 7 step scale going from least to most with 3 being the middle.

The *Gramaticallity is to be understood as a measurement of weather the poetry adhere to your intuitions about grammar
- is there anything in the poetry that sounds like its not grammatically correct (ie. is there a verb where a noun should be etc.)?
If nothing - or close to nothing - is grammatically readable give a low score, and vice versa.

The *Poeticness is to be understood as the semantics or the content of the poem. Does it make sense
- does each line come together to form a whole - is there a hidden or direkt meaning in the verse.
If the words are made up or if nothing - or close to nothing - makes any sense, or gives any meaning, give a low score, and vice versa.

*You can see these definitions again by hovering over the text in the experiment.
OBS! After each guess you will be presented with the correct answer in the bottom of the screen.
but note that this is not a competition! So no cheating and looking up if the poetry was by Milton or not
- Only honest answers help science
